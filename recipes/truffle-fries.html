Truffle Fries

Description

Parmesan truffle fries are a simple recipe that combines white truffle oil, parmesan cheese, and French fries. White truffle oil is a neutral oil, like canola, or safflower oil, that has been infused with white truffle peelings. In fact, you will usually see a small white truffle shaving at the bottom of the oil.

Truffle fries serve as a great side dish to a myriad of meals and will definitely impress guests and family with these simple extra steps to greatly enhance the flavor of regular French fries.

Ingredients

  • French fries
  • Truffle oil
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Wash and cut the potatoes into thick batonnet like slices.
  2. Rinse the potatoes well washing off as much starch as possible.
  3. Add the fries to hot oil in a pan or in a deep fryer at 300° for 3 minutes. Shake after 90 seconds.
  4. Remove the fries and set them aside until ready to serve them. Turn the heat up until the oil reaches 350°.
  5. When it comes time to serve the fries, this can be done right away, add the pre-fried fries to the fryer a few handfuls at a time and cook for 5-6 minutes or until the edges are browned. Feel free to cook longer for more browning.
  6. Drain the fries and season them with truffle oil, parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
